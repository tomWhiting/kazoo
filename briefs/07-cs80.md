# CS80 — Polyphonic Synthesizer

## What It Is

A polyphonic synthesizer inspired by the Yamaha CS-80. The synth behind Blade Runner, Toto's "Africa", Vangelis. Dual-layer architecture, 8-voice polyphony, ring modulation, aftertouch response. Fat, warm, cinematic. Not a clone — a spiritual descendant that captures the character.

## What It Does

### Architecture

Two independent synth layers (I and II), mixed per-voice. Each voice gets both layers. This is what gives the CS-80 its thickness — two slightly different timbres stacked.

```
Layer I:  Osc → HPF → LPF → Amp → ┐
                                     ├── Ring Mod (optional) → Voice Mix → Output
Layer II: Osc → HPF → LPF → Amp → ┘
```

### Per-Layer Signal Path

Each layer has its own complete signal chain:

**Oscillator:**
- Waveforms: sawtooth, square, sine, pulse (with PWM)
- Pulse width: 0.1 to 0.9 (for pulse wave)
- Sub-oscillator: one octave down, level controllable
- Feet (octave): 16', 8', 4', 2'

**Filters (dual per layer):**
- **HPF** — Resonant high-pass, 12dB/oct. Sweeps out low end. Key to the CS-80 character — most synths only have low-pass.
- **LPF** — Resonant low-pass, 12dB/oct. Standard subtractive shaping.
- Both filters have independent cutoff, resonance, and envelope modulation amount.

**Envelopes (per layer):**
- Filter envelope — ADSR, modulates both HPF and LPF cutoff.
- Amp envelope — ADSR, controls voice amplitude.
- The original CS-80 had DADSR (initial level + ADSR). We simplify to standard ADSR.

### Voice Management

- **8-voice polyphony.** Voices are dynamically allocated.
- **Voice stealing:** When all 8 voices are active and a new note arrives, steal the oldest voice in release phase. If none in release, steal the oldest active voice.
- **Unison mode:** All 8 voices on one note, detuned. The "super saw" wall-of-sound mode.
- **Detune spread:** In unison, voices are spread ± N cents. Configurable.

### Ring Modulation

Multiplies Layer I and Layer II oscillator outputs together. Creates metallic, bell-like, inharmonic tones. Mix control blends between normal layer sum and ring-modulated signal.

### Aftertouch / Expression

The real CS-80 was famous for polyphonic aftertouch — each key sensed pressure independently. We support:

- **Channel aftertouch** — Single pressure value modulates all voices equally.
- **Velocity** — Note-on velocity scales amplitude and optionally filter cutoff.
- **Aftertouch → vibrato** — Pressure adds pitch vibrato (LFO depth).
- **Aftertouch → brilliance** — Pressure opens filter cutoff.

### LFO

- Waveforms: sine, triangle, square, sample-and-hold (random)
- Rate: 0.1 Hz to 30 Hz
- Destinations: pitch (vibrato), filter cutoff, amplitude (tremolo), pulse width
- Depth controllable per destination
- Sync to Pulse clock optional

### Chorus / Ensemble

The CS-80's built-in chorus is a huge part of its sound. A simple stereo chorus with:

- Rate, depth, mix controls
- Two delay lines with modulated read positions
- This is NOT the `fx` crate chorus — it's baked into the instrument for the right character.

## Dependencies

- `dsp` crate (oscillators, envelopes, SVF/HPF filters, chorus delay lines)
- `pulse` crate (clock sync for LFO, if synced)
- `serde` + `serde_json`
- `hound` (via dsp)

## Testing

- Single note, single layer: verify clean tone at correct pitch.
- Dual layer: render both layers, verify output is richer than either alone.
- Polyphony: play 8-note chord, verify all 8 notes audible in FFT.
- Voice stealing: play 9 notes with 8-voice limit, verify oldest note is replaced.
- Unison: play single note in unison mode, verify thicker sound with detune.
- Ring mod: play two layers with ring_mod_mix=1.0, verify inharmonic content.
- Chorus: compare dry vs wet output, verify stereo widening.
- Long pad: render a slow Blade Runner-style pad, output WAV for listening.
- Aftertouch: apply velocity=0.3 vs velocity=1.0, verify amplitude and brightness difference.
